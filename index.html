• 1. The trident was the signature architectural element at the Twin Towers' base. It has become a symbol of strength and resilience.
  • 2. The 17.76-degree angle reflects the 1,776-foot height (with spire) of 1 World Trade Center.
  • 3. The spaces between the three bars echo the light beams of the Tribute in Light, an art installation that created two columns of light in remembrance of the 9/11 attacks.
  • 4. The letter W is an abbreviation for both the World Trade Center and the Westfield World Trade Center, a luxury shopping center.
  • 5. The pools of water at the National September 11 Memorial where the towers stood.
  • 6. The five towers on the 16-acre site: 7 World Trade Center; 1 and 4 World Trade Center, almost complete; 3 World Trade Center, now under construction; and 2 World Trade Center, in the planning stages.
